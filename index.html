About

Rugby is the national sport of New Zealand. The national rugby team, representing men's international rugby union, is commonly known as the All Blacks. The All Blacks played their first test match in 1903, and since then have become famous for their prolific international success and are considered one of the most successful sports teams in human history.

Known For

Prior to every international match, the All Blacks perform a Haka, or Maori dance. The tradition has been associated with New Zealand rugby since an 1888 tour. The most performed Haka is called "Ka mate, Ka mate." In August 2005, the team performed a new Haka, "Kapa O Pango," celebrating the land of New Zealand, the silver fern (the national symbol), and its warriors in black.

Learn more about the Haka from the New Zealand All Blacks website.
